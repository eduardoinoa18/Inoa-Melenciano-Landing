Important Legal Disclosure

We provide administrative assistance with USCIS form completion—helping you organize documents, understand form requirements, and submit accurate applications. We are not immigration attorneys and do not provide legal advice, legal representation, or appear before USCIS on your behalf.

For complex cases, deportation defense, waivers, or legal interpretation, please consult a licensed immigration attorney.

Common Immigration Forms We Help With

N-400 (Citizenship Application)

Who needs it: Lawful permanent residents (green card holders) applying for U.S. citizenship.

What we do: Help complete form, compile supporting documents, review eligibility requirements, prepare for interview.

I-130 (Family Petition)

Who needs it: U.S. citizens or permanent residents petitioning for qualifying family members.

What we do: Organize family documents, verify relationships, prepare petition package, track processing.

I-485 (Adjustment of Status)

Who needs it: Individuals in the U.S. applying for permanent residence (green card).

What we do: Compile employment/family sponsorship docs, complete medical forms, organize financial evidence.

I-765 (Work Permit / EAD)

Who needs it: Applicants for employment authorization documents (temporary work permits).

What we do: Verify eligibility category, complete application, track renewal deadlines.

I-90 (Green Card Renewal)

Who needs it: Permanent residents renewing or replacing expired/lost green cards.

What we do: Update information, prepare photos, handle fee payment documentation.

I-131 (Travel Document / Re-Entry Permit)

Who needs it: Permanent residents planning extended travel outside the U.S.

What we do: Explain travel restrictions, complete application, schedule biometrics.

Step-by-Step Process: How We Assist You

1

Initial Consultation

What happens: We discuss your immigration goal (citizenship, green card, family petition, work permit, etc.) and assess eligibility.

What you bring: Current immigration documents (green card, visa, passport), previous USCIS notices, marriage/birth certificates if applicable.

Duration: 30-45 minutes

2

Document Collection & Review

What happens: We provide a detailed checklist of required documents based on your specific form (IDs, financial records, employment letters, tax returns, proof of relationship).

What you do: Gather all requested documents. We review for completeness and accuracy, identify missing items.

Duration: 1-2 weeks (depends on document availability)

3

Form Preparation & Completion

What happens: We sit with you to complete the USCIS form accurately in English, translating questions into Spanish as needed. We ensure consistency across all fields.

What you do: Provide accurate answers to biographical, residence, employment, and travel history questions.

Duration: 1-2 hours per form

4

Final Review & Signature

What happens: We review the completed application line-by-line with you, verify supporting documents are properly organized and labeled.

What you do: Sign and date the form. We prepare copies for your records.

Duration: 30-60 minutes

5

Package Assembly & Filing

What happens: We organize all forms, documents, photos, and payment (check/money order) into a complete USCIS package with cover letter and checklist.

What you do: Provide filing fee payment. We mail via certified mail with tracking or guide you to file online if eligible.

Duration: Same day or next business day

6

Follow-Up & Interview Prep

What happens: We track your receipt notice, monitor processing times, explain next steps (biometrics, interview). For N-400 citizenship, we provide interview preparation and civics test practice.

What you do: Notify us of any USCIS correspondence. Attend scheduled appointments.

Duration: Ongoing until case completion

Typical Documents Required (Varies by Case)

Identity & Status Documents

  • Valid passport & travel documents
  • Current green card (if applicable)
  • Birth certificate (translated if not in English)
  • Marriage certificate / divorce decrees
  • Previous USCIS approval notices
  • Government-issued photo ID

Financial & Employment

  • Tax returns (past 3-5 years)
  • W-2s, 1099s, pay stubs
  • Employment verification letters
  • Bank statements
  • Proof of financial support (I-864 for family petitions)

Residence & Travel History

  • Proof of residence (lease, mortgage, utility bills)
  • Complete address history for past 5 years
  • Travel records & passport stamps
  • Children's birth certificates (if applicable)

Case-Specific Documents

  • Medical examination (I-693 for adjustment of status)
  • Police clearances (if required)
  • Passport-style photos (USCIS specifications)
  • Proof of relationship (for family petitions)
  • Educational diplomas/transcripts (if needed)
