Tax Season Preparation

We provide professional tax preparation services for individuals and businesses. Our team stays current with IRS regulations and state requirements to ensure accurate filings and maximum allowable deductions.

Note: While we prepare tax returns with care, we recommend consulting a licensed CPA or tax attorney for complex tax matters, audits, or legal tax advice.

Services Included

  • Personal federal and state tax returns (1040, schedules A/C/E).
  • Business tax filings (sole proprietor Schedule C, LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp).
  • Rental property income and expense reporting with depreciation calculations.
  • Self-employment and freelancer guidance including quarterly estimated payments.
  • Year-round tax planning and deduction optimization strategies.

Step-by-Step Tax Preparation Process

1

Initial Tax Consultation

What happens: We discuss your tax situation—employment type (W-2, 1099, business owner), income sources, life changes (marriage, kids, home purchase), deductions, previous tax issues.

What you bring: Last year's tax return, current year income documents, questions about deductions.

Duration: 30-45 minutes

Cost: Free consultation

2

Document Collection & Organization

What happens: We provide a personalized checklist based on your situation. You gather all income documents (W-2s, 1099s, business records), deduction receipts, and supporting documents.

Timeline: 1-2 weeks (we work on your schedule)

What we need: All income sources, expense receipts, last year's return, bank/investment statements, mortgage interest, property tax, charitable donations, business expenses.

3

Data Entry & Initial Review

What happens: We input all your information into professional tax software, verify accuracy, check for missing items, flag potential issues or audit risks.

Duration: 2-3 business days

What we check: Math accuracy, proper reporting, consistency across forms, compliance with IRS rules.

4

Deduction Optimization & Strategy Review

What happens: We identify all eligible deductions and credits you qualify for. We compare standard deduction vs itemizing. We explain tax-saving strategies for next year.

Common deductions we maximize: Home office, business mileage, retirement contributions, education expenses, medical costs, charitable giving, childcare, energy credits.

Duration: 1 day

5

Client Review & Signature

What happens: We sit with you to review the completed return line-by-line. We explain your refund or balance due, answer all questions, discuss payment options if you owe, prepare state returns.

What you do: Review for accuracy, ask questions, sign and date Form 8879 (e-file authorization).

Duration: 30-60 minutes

6

E-Filing & Confirmation

What happens: We electronically file your federal and state returns with the IRS and state agency. You receive confirmation within 24-48 hours. We provide copies for your records.

Refund timing: 10-21 days for direct deposit, 4-6 weeks for paper check.

Follow-up: We're available if IRS/state has questions or you're selected for audit support.

Document Checklist by Tax Type

Personal Income (W-2 / 1099)

  • All W-2 forms from employers
  • 1099-NEC, 1099-MISC (contract work)
  • 1099-INT, 1099-DIV (interest, dividends)
  • 1099-R (retirement distributions)
  • 1099-G (unemployment, state refunds)
  • 1099-B (stock sales)
  • 1099-K (payment apps over $600)
  • SSA-1099 (Social Security income)

Business Owners / Self-Employed

  • Profit & Loss statement (P&L)
  • Business income records (all 1099s, sales)
  • Business expense receipts
  • Mileage log (business use of vehicle)
  • Home office measurements (if applicable)
  • Equipment/asset purchases
  • Quarterly estimated tax payments
  • Business bank/credit card statements

Deductions & Credits

  • Mortgage interest (Form 1098)
  • Property tax bills
  • Charitable donation receipts
  • Medical/dental expenses over 7.5% AGI
  • Student loan interest (Form 1098-E)
  • Tuition payments (Form 1098-T)
  • Childcare provider info (EIN, address)
  • Energy-efficient home improvements

Rental Property Owners

  • Rental income records
  • Property expenses (maintenance, repairs)
  • Mortgage interest & property tax
  • Insurance premiums
  • HOA fees
  • Depreciation schedule (we calculate)
  • Property management fees
  • Utility bills (if landlord-paid)

Important Tax Deadlines 2025

January 31, 2025

W-2 & 1099 Deadline: Employers must send W-2s and 1099s to workers. Start gathering your documents!

April 15, 2025

Individual Tax Return Deadline: File Form 1040 for 2024 tax year. Also deadline for first quarter 2025 estimated tax payment (self-employed).

June 16, 2025

Q2 Estimated Tax Payment: Second quarter estimated tax payment due for self-employed individuals and businesses.

September 15, 2025

Q3 Estimated Tax Payment: Third quarter estimated tax payment due. Also extension deadline for S-Corps and Partnerships.

October 15, 2025

Extension Deadline: Final deadline for individual tax returns if you filed for a 6-month extension in April.

January 15, 2026

Q4 Estimated Tax Payment: Fourth quarter 2025 estimated tax payment due. Start preparing for 2025 tax season!
